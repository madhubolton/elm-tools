Existential Load Model — Applied Analysis

The Carbon Machine

Fossil fuel companies, their shareholders, and the financial architecture of organised delay
The climate crisis is not a failure of knowledge. Since at least the late 1970s, the organisations most responsible for carbon emissions have possessed internal scientific models of extraordinary accuracy — models that correctly predicted the warming trajectory the world is now living inside. What happened next is the subject of this analysis: how a system with accurate prediction chose to weaponise uncertainty rather than act on what it knew, and what load architecture made that choice not just possible but structurally inevitable. The Existential Load Model does not replace the legal, political, and historical accounts of fossil fuel industry behaviour. It adds a specific layer: a structural explanation of why the system generated this behaviour, how it has sustained it across decades, and why conventional pressure has so consistently failed to resolve it.
On Method, Evidence, and Interpretation

The factual claims in this analysis are drawn from peer-reviewed research, congressional investigations, and documented internal industry communications. Key sources include the 2023 Science paper by Geoffrey Supran, Naomi Oreskes, and Stefan Rahmstorf quantifying ExxonMobil's internal climate projections; the 2024 joint congressional staff report "Denial, Disinformation, and Doublespeak" produced by the Senate Budget Committee and House Oversight Committee; the Union of Concerned Scientists' Climate Deception Dossiers; and InsideClimate News's foundational 2015 investigation. Where specific companies are named, the claims are grounded in documented evidence from those sources. The ELM analysis that sits on top of these facts is interpretive — it offers a structural account of why the documented behaviour occurred and has persisted, not a substitute for the empirical record. This is analysis and interpretation through a theoretical lens, not advocacy. The lens is applied with the same structural logic to all actors in the system, including those whose behaviour is more sympathetic.

Part One
Actor Load Profiles
THE MAJOR INTEGRATED OIL COMPANIES
Primary load: Identity Room → Prediction Room (split)
IDENTITY / PREDICTION
ExxonMobil, Shell, BP, Chevron, TotalEnergies — each carries an Identity Room built over a century around extraction as civilisation-enabling heroism. These are not merely profitable businesses; they are organisations whose self-understanding is inseparable from the narrative of powering the modern world. Energy security, economic development, the heating of homes and the fuelling of economies — this meaning architecture is deeply embedded in corporate culture, workforce identity, and public positioning.

The Prediction Room presents the system's most diagnostically significant feature: a structural split between two simultaneously operating models — one private, one public. Internal scientific teams from the late 1970s onward generated climate projections of documented accuracy. A 2023 peer-reviewed analysis in Science found that ExxonMobil's scientists between 1977 and 2003 accurately projected warming with 63–83% skill, comparable to or exceeding independent academic models of the same period. The public Prediction Room — what these companies told regulators, investors, and the public — was systematically contradicted by that internal science.

This is not straightforwardly hypocrisy. Through the ELM it is something more structurally interesting: the Prediction Room split is a load-management mechanism. If the internal prediction were publicly acknowledged, the Identity Room would face immediate reorganisation demands — the civilisation-enabling heroism narrative collapses when the product is a confirmed planetary threat. Maintaining public uncertainty preserves the Identity Room while allowing the private Prediction Room to continue informing capital allocation decisions. In 1989, for example, Exxon used its own accurate climate projections to justify planning Arctic operations — confident the ice would recede. In 1999, the same company's CEO publicly called climate projections "based on completely unproven climate models."
SYSTEM LOAD
INSTITUTIONAL SHAREHOLDERS
Primary load: Prediction Room (temporal mismatch)
PREDICTION / AGENCY
Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, and index funds collectively constitute the largest class of fossil fuel shareholders — and present a load profile of distinctive incoherence. Many of these institutions are simultaneously invested in fossil fuel companies and in the physical assets that climate change will destroy. A pension fund holding ExxonMobil while also holding coastal real estate, agricultural land, and infrastructure bonds is running mutually invalidating predictions in the same portfolio.

The Prediction Room dysregulation is temporal in character: institutional investment operates on 3-5 year return horizons while climate change operates on 30-50 year damage horizons. The mismatch is not ignorance — most major institutional investors have climate risk assessment teams — but a structural inability to integrate long-timeline predictions into short-timeline decision frameworks. The Agency Room responds accordingly: engagement resolutions, net-zero commitments, ESG frameworks — all of which maintain the experience of agency (we are addressing this) while leaving the underlying capital allocation substantially unchanged.

The ELM identifies this as Agency Room performance substituting for Prediction Room integration. The investment thesis and the climate science cannot both be true over the relevant time horizon, and the system manages that incoherence through action that is visible without being transformative.
SYSTEM LOAD
THE FINANCIAL SYSTEM — BANKS & INSURERS
Primary load: Agency Room (compulsive optionality)
AGENCY / MEANING
The banks and insurers financing fossil fuel extraction are running a specific Agency Room pattern: compulsive optionality — maintaining maximum flexibility while the underlying conditions narrow. The world's 60 largest banks provided $6.9 trillion in fossil fuel financing between the Paris Agreement in 2016 and 2023. Net-zero banking alliances, climate pledges, and green financing frameworks run alongside continued fossil fuel lending — the verbal markers of a system asserting agency in two contradictory directions simultaneously.

Insurance companies present the sharpest version of this dynamic. Major insurers are withdrawing coverage from fossil fuel projects in some jurisdictions while continuing to underwrite them in others. They are simultaneously refusing to insure homes in climate-risk zones in California and Florida while underwriting the extraction operations generating the conditions that created those risk zones. The system is being acted upon by its own outputs — a closed loop of generated load that the Agency Room cannot coherently address because the Meaning Room has no framework for internalising that causal connection.

The Meaning Room fracture is significant: no coherent narrative has emerged within the financial sector about what its role in the energy transition actually means — whether it is a transition enabler, a capital allocator following market signals, or a party with causal responsibility for climate outcomes.
SYSTEM LOAD
TRADE ASSOCIATIONS — API, GCC & SUCCESSORS
Primary load: Meaning Room (organised inversion)
MEANING / AGENCY
The American Petroleum Institute, the Global Climate Coalition (active 1989–2002), and their successor organisations represent a distinct actor class: entities whose primary function is Meaning Room architecture for the broader fossil fuel system. They do not extract oil. They produce and distribute the narrative frameworks within which extraction is culturally and politically legible.

The GCC's internal documents show it understood the science it publicly undermined. A 1995 internal primer acknowledged that the greenhouse effect is "real" and "indisputably linked" to fossil fuel combustion — while the organisation simultaneously funded public communications casting exactly that link as uncertain. Congressional investigations documented that between 2000 and 2016, the fossil fuel industry spent more than $2 billion on congressional lobbying, outspending environmental organisations and the renewable energy sector by approximately 10 to one.

The ELM identifies this as a specific and novel pattern: organised Prediction Room noise production as a Meaning Room survival strategy. If the prediction is contested — if the science is publicly uncertain — then the Identity Room never has to update, the Meaning Room never has to reorganise, and the Agency Room can continue operating in its current configuration. Manufacturing doubt is not primarily a strategic communication choice; it is existential load management at industry scale.
SYSTEM LOAD
Part Two
The Split Prediction Room
The most structurally significant feature of the fossil fuel system is not denial — it is the simultaneous operation of two incompatible Prediction Rooms within the same organisations. This split is the generative mechanism from which all other load dynamics follow. Understanding it structurally is what the ELM adds to the documented historical record.
PRIVATE PREDICTION ROOM — INTERNAL SCIENCE
What the companies
knew and used
The 2023 Science paper by Supran, Oreskes, and Rahmstorf established with quantitative precision what investigative journalism had begun to reveal in 2015: ExxonMobil's internal scientists, from 1977 through 2003, generated global warming projections that were accurate to within the range of independent academic climate science of the same era. Their average projected warming of 0.20°C per decade, within stated uncertainty, matched what actually occurred. Their prediction that human-caused warming would first become statistically detectable around the year 2000 — stated internally in the early 1980s — proved correct.

ExxonMobil's average projected warming was 0.20 ± 0.04°C per decade — within uncertainty, the same as that of independent academic and government projections published between 1970 and 2007.
This private Prediction Room was operational and informing business decisions. By 1981, Exxon was factoring climate projections into planning for Arctic fossil fuel extraction — correctly anticipating that warming would reduce sea ice and open new drilling opportunities. The private Prediction Room was not suppressed; it was compartmentalised. It could inform capital allocation while being quarantined from public communications. This is the structural feature that makes the ELM account important: the split was not between knowing and not knowing, but between two functional uses of the same knowledge.
PUBLIC PREDICTION ROOM — ORGANISED UNCERTAINTY
What the companies said
and systematically funded
While internal science accurately modelled warming, the public communications of ExxonMobil and its peers — and the organisations they funded — ran a sustained campaign to represent that same science as uncertain, contested, and premature. The tactics were not improvised. A 1998 API internal memo outlined a plan to make "climate science uncertainty" a household concept, targeting schoolteachers and science journalists as key audiences.

A senior ExxonMobil lobbyist, caught on tape in 2021: "Did we aggressively fight against some of the science? Yes."
The tools were varied and systematic: funding of think tanks and front groups to produce alternative literature; advertising campaigns in major publications hyping scientific uncertainty; the funding of a small number of credentialed scientists willing to maintain public positions the broader scientific community had abandoned; and — documented in the congressional investigation — infiltration of regulatory and advisory bodies. Philip Cooney, a former API lobbyist, became chief of staff at the White House Council on Environmental Quality under the Bush administration, where government scientists documented him rewriting federal climate research reports to introduce doubt. He resigned in 2005 and immediately joined ExxonMobil.

The public Prediction Room was not a separate mistake from the private one. It was a deliberate engineering of the system's Prediction Room environment — designed to prevent the conditions under which the private knowledge would generate an obligation to act.
Part Three
The Four Rooms — Carbon Machine Mapping
I
PREDICTION ROOM
What will happen?
What do we actually know?
MAJORS TRADE ASSOC. FINANCIAL
The most sophisticated load-management strategy the ELM has encountered in any case study: the deliberate production of Prediction Room noise as an organisational survival mechanism. The fossil fuel system did not fail to understand climate science — it understood it with remarkable precision. What it engineered was a public Prediction Room environment in which that precision could not generate binding social or regulatory consequences.

The ELM illuminates why this strategy was structurally available: Prediction Room uncertainty is the only condition under which Identity Room reorganisation can be deferred indefinitely. As long as the prediction is contested, "we are responding appropriately to scientific uncertainty" remains a coherent Agency Room position. The moment the prediction achieves undeniable public salience — which it increasingly has — the deferral strategy exhausts itself and is replaced by the current phase: acknowledgement accompanied by insufficient action.
II
AGENCY ROOM
What are we doing?
Who is acting on whom?
MAJORS SHAREHOLDERS
The Agency Room across the fossil fuel system shows two distinct patterns running simultaneously: compulsive optionality in public communications, and highly coherent directional action in private capital allocation.

The public Agency Room is fractured: net-zero commitments, transition investment announcements, carbon capture pledges, Paris Agreement endorsements. A 2019 InfluenceMap report found that the five largest publicly traded oil and gas majors invested over $1 billion in the three years following the Paris Agreement on climate-related branding — while simultaneously lobbying against the climate policies the Agreement required. The private Agency Room is not fractured at all: continued upstream fossil fuel investment, long-term extraction contracts, and — in ExxonMobil's case — Arctic drilling projects planned explicitly on the basis of the warming the company was publicly denying. This is Agency Room coherence in service of Prediction Room concealment.
III
IDENTITY ROOM
Who are we?
What does our existence mean?
MAJORS TRADE ASSOC.
The Identity Room is the deepest load and the primary driver of every other room's dysregulation. These are organisations that have understood themselves for a century as the material foundation of modern civilisation — the entities that power economies, heat homes, enable mobility, and underwrite national security. That identity is not propaganda; it is structurally accurate as a description of what fossil fuels have been to industrial civilisation.

The threat climate science poses is not merely regulatory or financial. It is ontological: if the product is a planetary threat, the identity built around providing it collapses. The ELM predicts that Identity Room threats of this magnitude will generate the most extreme and sustained defensive responses available to a system — and the 40-year disinformation campaign is exactly that response. It is not primarily strategic; it is the system doing whatever is necessary to prevent having to become something other than what it has always been.
IV
MEANING ROOM
Why does this matter?
What does it serve?
MAJORS SHAREHOLDERS FINANCIAL
The Meaning Room across the fossil fuel system is fractured at every level — and this fracture is the condition that makes the current phase of "acknowledged-but-insufficient" action structurally stable.

No coherent meaning architecture has emerged within the industry or the financial system that makes the energy transition meaningfully urgent at the pace the science requires. "Affordable, reliable energy for growing economies" remains a coherent Meaning Room position for industry actors. "Long-term risk management" is a coherent Meaning Room position for investors. "Orderly transition" is a coherent Meaning Room position for governments. None of these generate the meaning-load that would produce Agency Room responses at the required speed. The ELM would predict exactly what is observable: technically sophisticated transition planning, net-zero commitments on timelines that conveniently exceed current leadership tenures, and continued capital flows into fossil fuel infrastructure that will remain operational for decades.
Part Four
Coupled Oscillator Dynamics
In the ELM, systems entrain to each other — a coherent oscillator stabilises adjacent dysregulated ones, while a dysregulated oscillator transmits its load outward. The carbon machine involves several distinct coupling patterns, most of which have been actively engineered rather than emergent. This is what makes the climate case structurally unique: some of the most consequential couplings were deliberately designed.
COUPLING DYNAMIC ELM PATTERN
Industry ↔ Public Prediction Room The most consequential engineered coupling in the system. By funding an ecosystem of think tanks, front groups, and credentialed contrarians — the Global Climate Coalition, the Heartland Institute, the George C. Marshall Institute, and dozens of others documented in the UCS Climate Deception Dossiers — the fossil fuel industry deliberately coupled its own dysregulated Prediction Room to the broader public's Prediction Room. The goal was entrainment: to transmit industry uncertainty into public consciousness, regulatory frameworks, and policy timelines. The 2015 congressional testimony of Naomi Oreskes established that this strategy used the same personnel, the same organisations, and the same tactics previously deployed by the tobacco industry — including funding scientists willing to maintain positions the scientific mainstream had abandoned. Deliberate dysregulation entrainment — engineering Prediction Room noise in an adjacent system to prevent regulatory consequences of accurate internal prediction. Structurally novel: load-management through the corruption of the adjacent system's epistemic architecture.
Capital ↔ Carbon Lock-In Every dollar of fossil fuel infrastructure investment creates a physical lock-in that extends for 20-40 years. A gas power plant built in 2024 will operate into the 2060s. An LNG terminal represents committed extraction contracts that outlast any political cycle. Capital allocation and physical infrastructure are coupled oscillators — once the capital flows, the physical system demands continued operation to justify it, which demands continued capital to maintain it, which demands continued regulatory frameworks to permit it. The carbon machine has built an extraordinarily robust coupling between the financial system and the physical extraction system that generates its own momentum independent of any actor's intention. No single actor decided to create a 40-year lock-in; the coupling emerged from individually rational investment decisions aggregated across the system. Emergent lock-in coupling — the feedback between capital investment and physical infrastructure creates an attractor that persists beyond any individual actor's decision-making horizon. Structural inertia as the primary stabiliser of the high-carbon attractor.
Industry ↔ Political System The $2 billion in congressional lobbying documented between 2000 and 2016 — outspending the renewable sector 10 to one — represents the most direct coupling in the system: the deliberate entrainment of the political system's Agency Room to the fossil fuel industry's identity and meaning architecture. The revolving door between industry and regulatory bodies (documented extensively in the congressional investigation) tightens this coupling further. The political system's Agency Room cannot generate climate policy at the pace required when its Prediction Room inputs are contaminated by industry-funded uncertainty and its key decision-makers carry industry-generated identity associations. This coupling explains why 40 years of scientific consensus has produced policy responses that consistently fall short of what the science indicates is necessary. Agency Room capture — the political system's primary response mechanism has been entrained to the industry's identity and meaning architecture, preventing the independent Agency Room responses the Prediction Room evidence would otherwise generate.
Shareholders ↔ Fiduciary Duty The fiduciary duty framework — the legal obligation of institutional investors to act in beneficiaries' financial interests — has historically been interpreted to require short-term return maximisation, creating a structural coupling between the legal system's meaning architecture and the fossil fuel industry's continued operation. Divesting from fossil fuels could be argued as a fiduciary breach in jurisdictions where short-term returns are the operative metric. This coupling has been the legal architecture within which the shareholder engagement strategy — resolution campaigns, net-zero pledges, engagement frameworks — has operated, and it has consistently limited that strategy to the pace of change that short-term return frameworks can absorb. The meaning structure of fiduciary law has been an inadvertent stabiliser of the carbon machine. Meaning-law coupling — the meaning architecture embedded in fiduciary law has constrained the Agency Room of the system's largest capital holders, creating a structural barrier to the pace of transition the Prediction Room evidence requires.
Greenwashing ↔ Identity Maintenance BP's 2000 rebrand — "Beyond Petroleum" — was the first major greenwashing campaign, and the ELM illuminates its structural function precisely. It was not primarily a marketing strategy. It was an Identity Room intervention: a reorganisation of the company's public self-description that allowed it to maintain coherence as climate salience increased, without requiring any underlying Agency Room change. Shell's website urging climate action while its internal guidance stated "please do not give the impression that Shell is willing to reduce carbon dioxide emissions to levels that do not make business sense" — revealed in the congressional investigation — is the same pattern at maximum compression. The greenwash is not cynicism failing to hide itself; it is Identity Room management generating the minimum Prediction Room and Agency Room changes required to maintain coherence under increasing external pressure. Identity maintenance through minimal Agency Room change — greenwashing as the precise quantum of adaptation required to preserve Identity Room coherence without triggering genuine Meaning Room reorganisation. The ELM predicts this pattern wherever external pressure increases faster than the system's willingness to genuinely update.

The absolute dismissal ("sheer speculation") is the signature of an Identity Room threat response, not a scientific assessment.

Part Six
The Novel ELM Finding
ELM THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION
Organised Prediction Room Noise
as Load-Management Strategy

Prior ELM analyses — the Iran conflict, the Tesla organisation — encountered Prediction Room dysregulation as a consequence of high load: actors under pressure generated distorted predictions as a byproduct of identity and meaning stress. The carbon machine case reveals something structurally different and theoretically significant: the deliberate, organised production of Prediction Room noise as a primary load-management tool.

In the Iran case, prediction errors emerged from ideological filtering — the system genuinely could not process information that threatened its identity architecture. In the fossil fuel case, the prediction was accurate internally while being deliberately contaminated externally. This is not prediction failure but prediction weaponisation: using manufactured epistemic uncertainty in the public Prediction Room as a barrier against the regulatory and social consequences that accurate public prediction would generate.

The ELM model had not previously needed to account for this configuration because it had not encountered a system with the resources, sophistication, and sustained motivation to engineer the Prediction Room environment of adjacent systems. The tobacco industry did this first; the fossil fuel industry refined and scaled it. The structural logic is identical: if the prediction cannot be publicly acknowledged without destroying the organisational identity, and if the organisation has sufficient resources to prevent public acknowledgement, then manufacturing epistemic uncertainty is the most efficient load-management strategy available.

This reveals an edge condition the ELM must account for: Prediction Room noise can be an output rather than only an input of load dynamics. Systems under sufficient Identity Room threat will, if they have the resources, attempt to engineer the Prediction Room environment of the systems they depend on. This is load externalisation at the epistemic level — the most sophisticated form of load-management the model has encountered, and the most consequential.

The second theoretical contribution: the carbon machine demonstrates that a system can maintain internal Prediction Room coherence while exporting dysfunction to the external Prediction Room. Exxon's capital allocation, Arctic planning, and risk assessment all used accurate climate science. The company's public communications, regulatory engagement, and political lobbying operated from manufactured uncertainty. The split is not cognitive dissonance in the psychological sense — it is functional compartmentalisation in service of Identity Room survival. The ELM must distinguish between systems whose Prediction Room is genuinely dysregulated and systems whose Prediction Room is strategically split.

Part Seven
System Diagnosis
INCOHERENCE PATTERN
Identity Survival Through
Epistemic Externalisation
The system's primary incoherence is the gap between private knowledge and public action — sustained for over four decades across multiple companies, trade associations, and political jurisdictions. The ELM diagnosis: this is not moral failure but load architecture. The Identity Room threat posed by accurate public climate prediction was existential for organisations whose entire self-understanding was built on the civilisation-enabling narrative. The resources to manage that threat through Prediction Room noise were available. The strategy was rationally available. The incoherence is the structure, not an aberration within it.
ROOT LOAD
Identity Room Threat
of Civilisational Scale
The generative condition is an Identity Room threat that is genuinely civilisational in scale — not just for fossil fuel companies but for the consumer economies, national energy systems, and development models built on their products. The carbon machine persists not because its actors are uniquely malevolent but because the Identity Room threat its accurate prediction would generate is distributed across the entire industrialised world. Every shareholder, every pension beneficiary, every economy dependent on fossil fuel infrastructure is an indirect participant in the Identity Room that accurate climate prediction threatens.
THE CURRENT PHASE
From Denial to Delay —
Prediction Room Exhaustion
The manufactured uncertainty strategy is structurally exhausted — not because it failed, but because the physical evidence of warming has become too pervasive to maintain. The industry has moved from Prediction Room denial to Meaning Room management: acknowledging the science while constructing meaning frameworks (orderly transition, natural gas as bridge fuel, carbon capture as salvation) that preserve the Identity Room and defer the Agency Room changes the Prediction Room evidence requires. This phase is more sophisticated than denial and harder to address through conventional pressure.
THE COHERENT OSCILLATOR GAP
No External System Stable
Enough to Provide Regulation
In the ELM, stabilisation requires a coherent external oscillator — a system stable enough to provide regulating entrainment. In the carbon machine, no actor in the system currently occupies this role with sufficient coherence and authority. International climate institutions (IPCC, UNFCCC) provide Prediction Room clarity but have no Agency Room capacity. National governments have Agency Room capacity but fractured Meaning Rooms. The financial system has resources but has been entrained to the industry's identity architecture. The coherent oscillator gap is the structural explanation for why scientific consensus has not generated commensurate policy action.
Synthesis
What the Model Reveals
01
THE CORE FINDING
This is not a story about knowledge failure. It is a story about Identity Room survival.
The fossil fuel system possessed accurate climate knowledge for decades. The organised campaign against public acknowledgement of that knowledge was not a product of ignorance but of a structurally predictable response to Identity Room threat. The ELM would have predicted this behaviour from the load architecture alone, without access to the internal documents.
02
THE NOVEL MECHANISM
Prediction Room noise as engineered load-management extends the ELM theoretically.
Prior analyses encountered distorted prediction as a byproduct of load. This case reveals it as a deliberate primary strategy. Systems under sufficient Identity Room threat, with sufficient resources, will attempt to engineer the Prediction Room environment of the systems they depend on. This is a new ELM pattern: load externalisation at the epistemic level.
03
THE CURRENT TRAP
The denial phase is over. The delay phase is structurally harder to address.
Manufactured uncertainty was addressable through sufficiently overwhelming empirical evidence — which eventually arrived. Meaning Room management (orderly transition, bridge fuels, carbon capture) is not addressable through evidence alone because it is not making empirical claims. It is constructing frameworks within which insufficient action appears reasonable. Different room, different intervention required.
04
THE DISTRIBUTED IDENTITY PROBLEM
The carbon machine's Identity Room is not contained within the industry.
The civilisational identity built on fossil fuel consumption extends through shareholders, pension beneficiaries, consumer economies, and national development models. Disrupting the carbon machine means disrupting identity structures distributed across the entire industrialised world — not just within the industry. This is why the transition, when it comes, will be experienced as identity reorganisation at civilisational scale, not merely an energy technology substitution.
05
THE PREDICTIVE CLAIM
Transition at the required pace cannot be achieved through Agency Room pressure alone.
The ELM generates a falsifiable prediction: as long as the Identity Room threat of rapid decarbonisation remains unaddressed — as long as no coherent alternative identity architecture is available for fossil fuel companies, fossil fuel communities, and fossil fuel economies — no amount of regulatory, financial, or market pressure will produce transition at the pace the Prediction Room evidence requires. The operative room is the Identity Room. It is not currently being addressed.
06
WHAT COMES NEXT
Part II maps the geopolitics — where Identity Room threat operates at national scale.
The corporate case is the most documented. The national case — oil-producing states whose sovereign identity, economic architecture, and domestic political legitimacy are built on fossil fuel extraction — presents the Identity Room threat in a form that is simultaneously more acute and less addressable through market mechanisms. For petrostate governments, the energy transition is not an economic disruption. It is an existential identity event.
